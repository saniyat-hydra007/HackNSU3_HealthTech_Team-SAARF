ACID BURN:

Call 999 or seek immediate care for major burns,
which burns are deep
Cause the skin to be dry and leathery
May appear charred or have patches of white, brown or black
Are larger than 3 inches (about 8 centimeters) in diameter or cover the hands, feet, face, groin, buttocks or a major joint

******If burn does not have any of the criteria above, follow these rules:

1.Remove the cause of the burn.
2.Remove clothing or jewelry that has been contaminated by the chemical.
3.WASH the chemical off the skin with cool running water for at least 10 minutes.
4.For dry chemicals, brush off any remaining material before flushing.
5. Wear gloves or use a towel or other suitable object, such as a brush.
6. Cover the burn with a sterile gauze bandage (not fluffy cotton) or Flusha clean cloth. Wrap it loosely to avoid putting pressure on burned skin.
7.Flush again if needed.
8. If you experience increased burning after the initial flushing, flush the burn area with water again for several more minutes.
9.Go to hospital as soon as possible.
